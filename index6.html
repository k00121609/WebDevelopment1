Feature Article: How to Play (January 2017)

by Sinéad Boyce

Lottery Games available are:

Lotto:

Pick any 6 numbers from 1 to 47. Minimum play is 2 panels per draw, costing 4 euros and you can also play additional panels for 2 euro per panel. The structure of the draw is that: 6 random numbers are drawn with a bonus number. You can play by: choosing your own numbers; by choosing Quick Pick numbers, by Playing Online. The draws are held on Wednesdays and Saturdays and are live on RTE One at approx. 8pm. Minimum jackpot is 2 million euros and increases upwards from there if the jackpot is not won. You can also play this game online. There is also another way of playing called ‘Lotto Advance Play.’ Here, you can play up to a month’s worth of Lotto draws in advance. Terminals closed at 7:45pm and reopen only when the 7:58pm draw is complete. Online play closes at 7:40pm.

Lotto Plus:

There are 2 games in this Lotto Plus: Lotto Plus 1 and Lotto Plus 2. For 50 cents per panel, you can play. The top prize for the former is 300 euro and later is 250,000 euro. Additionally, on purchase of a Lotto Plus ticket you will be automatically entered into the Lotto Plus Raffle Draw where the prize is 300euro. You receive a 4-digit Raffle number for every line of Lotto Plus you play. The draws are held every Wednesday and Saturday and are live on RTE One at approx. 8pm. You can also play this game online. There is also another way of playing called ‘Lotto Advance Play.’ Here, you can play up to a month’s worth of Lotto draws in advance. Terminals closed at 7:45pm and reopen only when the 7:58pm draw is complete. Online play closes at 7:40pm.

EuroMillions:

This is a lottery game where there are participants from various European countries. To play, you select 5 numbers between 1-50 and two random Lucky Star numbers from 1-12. Each line costs 2.50 euro and the minimum play is one line per draw. You can play by: choosing your own numbers; by choosing Quick Pick numbers, by Playing Online. There are substantial winnings where the minimum jackpot is 17 million euros. If not one this amount increases for the next draw. The days the draws are held are Tuesdays and Fridays. There is also a new aspect to this game called ‘Ireland Only Raffle,’ where, for each EuroMillions line you play you will get a nine-character Raffle code and if your ticket matches the winning draw code, the prize is 5,000 euro. Additionally, for every draw there will be 10 Irish winners. You can play by: choosing you own numbers; choosing quick pick numbers; playing online. Terminals close at 7:30pm and reopen at 7am the next morning. Online play closes at 7:25pm.

EuroMillions Plus:

You can play this game when you play EuroMillions by having your first 5 numbers (but not your lucky star numbers) entered into an Ireland only ‘EuroMillions Plus’ draw. Draws are held every Tuesdays and Fridays. You play after marking your EuroMillions ticket by marking the ‘Plus box’ on the top left-hand corner of your Euromillions ticket. Terminals close at 7:30pm and reopen at 7am the next morning. Online play closes at 7:25pm.

Daily Million:

Here you can select any 6 numbers from 1-39. In the draw 6 random numbers are picked and one bonus number. Each play costs 1 euro and the minimum play is one panel per draw. You can play by: choosing your own numbers; by choosing Quick Pick numbers, by Playing Online. Top prize is 1 million euro. Draws take place twice a day at 2pm and 9pm. Terminals close at 2pm and 9pm and reopen 10 minutes after each draw is complete.

Daily Million Plus:

There are draws twice a day for this game. Here once you have marked your numbers for the Daily Million draw, you mark the ‘Daily Million Plus box’ on the top left corner of your Daily Millions ticket. Terminals close at 2pm and 9pm and reopen 10 minutes after each draw is complete.

Lotto 5-4-3-2-1:

This is a game based on the results of Lotto, Lotto Pus 1 and Lotto Plus 2. You can play 1,2,3,4 or 5 numbers. These can be played on any or all of the above 3 lotto games. In each playslip there are 4 panels A, B, C, D. Panels A and B are for the 6 number games (Lotto, Lotto Plus 1, Lotto Plus 2) draws, and exclude the bonus number. Panels C and D are for the 7 number games (Lotto, Lotto Plus 1, Lotto Plus 2) draws including the bonus number. Top prize for the 6 number game per 1-euro play is 125,000 euro and for the 7 number game is 40,000 euro. Draws are held on Wednesdays and Saturdays. There are 2 main ways to play Lotto 5-4-3-2-1:

By choosing your own numbers:

  • Mark the box labelled ‘ALL 3 draws’ for to enter all three games. For to mark individual games only click which option you want from: Lotto, Lotto Plus 1 or Lotto Plus2, to indicate the particular game you wish to play.
  • Mark 1,2,3,4 or 5 to show how many numbers you wish to play per draw.
  • Mark 1euro, 2euro, 3euro, 4euro or 5euro to show the amount of money you wish to play per draw.
  • Finally, mark your selected numbers from 1-47.

By choosing Quick Pick numbers:

  • Follow steps as far as step ‘c’ above.
  • Mark the ‘Quick Pick box’ for to have random numbers selected for that panel.
  • Ask for Lotto 5-4-3-2-1 Quick Pick from you shop assistant.
Terminals close at 7:45pm and reopen at 7:58pm when the draw is finished.

Scratch Cards:

There are 22 Scratch card games. All you do is scratch the card as per ticket instructions. Top-Prizes range from between 300 euro and 25,000 euro. You can purchase a ticket from between 3euro and 10 euros.

Telly Bingo:

Here you can play Bingo in your own home. On EVERY Tuesday, Thursday and Friday. Sales on draw days close at 11pm and re-open at 11:15am. Each ticket costs 2.50euro and you can play in advance for up to 6 draws (2 weeks) in advance. All tickets are generated randomly by the Lotto terminal using Quick Pick.

Millionaire Raffle:

This is a limited Christmas draw with only 500,000 tickets. It is automated and takes place on New Year’s: last draw was on Saturday 31ST December 2016 and results were made available for on the 1st January 2017. Tickets can be checked at National Lottery Agents. Prizes range from 500euro up to 100,000euro.
