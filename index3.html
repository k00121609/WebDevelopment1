Feature Article: What are the recent 2016-2017 popular stories about Lotto winners? (August 2016)

by Sinéad Boyce

Dublin Bus syndicate wins €23.8 million Euro Millions jackpot:

A syndicate of 22 bus drivers from Dublin which have over 700 years’ service between they collected their winnings at National Lottery Headquarters accompanied by family and friends. They as a group have been playing the Lotto for 17 years and changed 3 years ago, from quick pick tickets to tickets using the numbers of their last quick pick ticket which was to deliver them a fortune.

There were over 550 other National Lottery prize winners for that draw on the 1st July 2016.


Friday the 13th Dublin business syndicate winners:

While the Chinese consider Friday the 13th a lucky day, it is not thought so by many people of Irish culture. Despite this, a Dublin office syndicate collected on Friday 13th of January their winnings of €250,000 that averaged out at almost €20,000 each.


Cavan family syndicate scoop Christmas Lotto Jackpot:

This was a great present for one Cavan family on Christmas morning of €4,577,663 in lottery winnings. On hearing that the ticket was sold as a Quick Pick in a local shop, the dad checked a ticket left in his car that he had purchased recently in the shop. His son then scanned the ticket on his phone and all were delighted to discover they had won a fortune. The family planned a big party for their neighbours and wish to keep their win private.


Kosovo Kerry man in wins ‘All Cash Spectacular’ scratch card:

Ljatif Saiti a Kerry man originally from Kosovo won a top scratch card win of €200,000 prize money. Ljatif has lived in Ireland since 1999 and is delighted with the win. When he scratched the ticket in the shop and saw the numbers matching up, Ljatif said that he quickly handed it back to the shopkeeper to check that he was not dreaming. Ljatif intends to enjoy the win first and then along with his family decide what to spend the money on. He says their children’s future is their priority.


Donegal Christmas ‘Millionaire Raffle’ winner:

The New Year’s draw on 31st December 2016 saw a Donegal man who wishes to keep his identity private win the second highest prize of €250,000 in the ‘Millionaire Raffle’ draw. He says that it was one of his better impulse buys while shopping. He has decided to pay his mortgage off first and with a household party planned and a holiday for all the family, there’s good fortune on the horizon in 2017 for this Donegal man.


Dublin student with 60cent left wins €100,00 Millionaire Raffle prize:

The student, a recent graduate, bought the winning ticket on the 6th of December and stated that they winnings could not have come at a better time with only 60cent left in his bank account after the Christmas. He wishes to remain anonymous. As well as purchasing a car, once he gets his driving license, he is going to surprise his girlfriend and family with a short holiday to celebrate properly.


Couple give lotto win to grandchildren:

A generous couple from Co. Meath are giving their Lotto win of €172,223 to be divided amongst their grandchildren. While having a coffee in bed on a Sunday morning the winner heard the Lotto numbers over the radio. Recognising these she said nothing to her husband until she had checked her ticket for sure. After mass, she checked her ticket in the shop and was surprised and delighted when she heard it was a winning ticket. The couple have decided to give all the money to their grand-children who are in school and college now. As the couple said their grandchildren are only starting out in life and will in the future need the money more than they would.


Army syndicate from Kilkenny win big in the Lotto draw:

20 soldiers from the 3rd Infantry Battalion wins €452,700 in the Lotto draw. The draw was held on 27th August 2016 where 5 numbers and a bonus was matched. The group kept to the same numbers in playing the lotto. Clive Parsons, a syndicate member, thinking on receipt of an e-mail from the National Lottery that the winnings would be a few euro, checked it the following day while browsing through his mail, to find the winning amount. Daniel McGrath the syndicate spokesman has said that the monies would be evenly divided between all syndicate members. Also, that the money will be useful as there are some life plans in many of the soldier’s lives like buying houses, getting married, family and children to look after.


Wicklow syndicate Halloween win:

A 24-member syndicate won €156,002 between them in the Euromillions draw on 25th October 2016. Aged in their late thirty’s and above they work in the County Wicklow Partnership group. They have been playing the Lotto since 2009 and the winning ticket was a quick-pick. On hearing there was a local win syndicate spokesperson Eibhlis Forde went back to the office and checked their ticket on the National Lottery App only to find confirmation of the big win. As Eibhlis says that it wil be a happy Christmas for all the team.


Perfect start to 2017 for a Dublin couple:

Shane Malone from Ballymun in Dublin and his girlfriend Emma are planning their future together wing winnings of €100,000 in the Millionaire Raffle draw. On scanning his ticket on the National Lottery App, he saw his prize money appear on-screen and checked his ticket a few times online to be certain. The couple had already planned a holiday away and the money will go towards their dream of buying a house together in the future.
